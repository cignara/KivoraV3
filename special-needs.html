Platform Features

Built-In Accessibility Features

🔊

Audio-First Design

Every activity has full audio narration. Children can use Kivora entirely by listening — no reading required for activities up to Grade 2.

🎯

Adjustable Pace

Parents can slow down activity pace and increase response time limits for children who need more processing time.

🎨

Visual Clarity

High-contrast colour options, dyslexia-friendly font mode, and simplified layouts available for children with visual processing differences.

🧩

Sensory-Friendly

No sudden loud sounds, no flashing animations, no timed pressure by default. All time-limits can be disabled by the parent.

📊

Granular Reporting

Track micro-progress for children who advance slowly. Every interaction logged so you can celebrate small wins meaningfully.

❤️

SEL & Emotional Support

Life Skills Village covers emotional regulation, mindfulness and social scenarios — essential for children with ADHD, autism and anxiety.

🏆

Flexible Rewards

The XP and badge system can be customised. Parents can set lower thresholds to celebrate smaller achievements and build confidence.

👨‍👩‍👧

Parent-Teacher Collaboration

Shared child progress data means parents and support teachers see the same picture and can coordinate learning goals.

By Profile

Supports a Range of Learning Profiles

Every child is unique. These are common ways Kivora adapts — but many features combine to support the full spectrum of learners.

🎵

Autism Spectrum

Predictable structure, no social pressure, sensory-friendly defaults, and deep subject exploration for areas of special interest.

🧠

ADHD

Short activities (3–7 minutes), immediate reward feedback, gamified XP system, and parent-set time limits to support focus.

📖

Dyslexia

Audio narration for all text, phonics support in Alphabet Island, visual word cards, and optional dyslexia-friendly font.

👁️

Visual Impairment

High-contrast mode, large target areas for touch/click, audio descriptions in all activities. Screen reader support in development.

❤️

Anxiety & Emotional Regulation

Life Skills Village SEL content, no competitive elements by default, mindfulness videos, and adjustable difficulty to build confidence.

🦽

Motor Differences

Large touch targets, no drag-required activities (alternatives provided), keyboard navigation, and adjustable timing.

📋

Coming Soon: Special Needs Dashboard

A dedicated parent panel with IEP-friendly progress tracking, sensory preference settings, and recommended activity sequences built by SEN specialists.
